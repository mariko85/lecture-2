Favorite Fried Eggs

>he crispy olive oil fried egg. These fried eggs have golden, lacy, crispy edges, which contrast beautifully with their runny yellow yolks. Contrast is everything! Start with a good egg, a hot skillet, and a generous drizzle of olive oil, and you’ll end up with the most flavorful fried egg you’ve ever tasted. They are infinitely better than t he rubbery, sulfurous eggs that come out of non-stick skillets. In fact, these are the only fried eggs I ever want to eat.

Ingredients (for 2 servings):

  • 3 eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of milk (optional – for a softer texture)
  • Black pepper – to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of butter or oil

Instructions:

  1. Whisk the eggs: In a bowl, beat the eggs with the milk. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Heat the pan: Melt the butter or heat the oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  3. Pour the mixture: Pour the egg mixture into the hot pan. Tilt the pan slightly to spread it evenly.
  4. Add fillings (optional): As the eggs start to set, sprinkle your desired fillings evenly over the surface.
  5. Fold the omelet:When the bottom is set and the top is still slightly runny, gently fold the omelet in half.
  6. Finish cooking: Cook for a few more seconds, then slide the omelet onto a plat
